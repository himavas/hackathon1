Description

Bhangra is a high-energy folk dance that originated in the Punjab region of India and Pakistan, historically performed during the harvest festival of Vaisakhi. It has now evolved into a global dance phenomenon, popular at weddings, celebrations, and dance competitions worldwide. Bhangra is characterized by: Powerful jumps and shoulder movements that reflect the joyous spirit of Punjabi farmers. Rhythmic beats of the dhol (a traditional double-headed drum), which drive the dance. Fast-paced footwork and hand gestures that add to its dynamic appeal. Traditional bhangra outfits include vibrant turbans, flowing kurtas, and colorful dupattas, representing Punjabi culture. Today, bhangra has been fused with Western dance styles, giving rise to Bhangra Hip-Hop and Bhangra Fusion, which mix traditional steps with modern beats.
