Description

Ballet is one of the most disciplined and structured dance forms, known for its grace, poise, and technical precision. Originating in the Italian Renaissance courts of the 15th century, it later flourished in France and Russia, where it was refined into the classical ballet style we know today. Ballet dancers undergo rigorous training to develop balance, flexibility, and control. Movements are executed with lightness and fluidity, often giving the illusion of floating. Some key elements of ballet include: Pointe Work: Dancing on the tips of the toes using specially designed shoes. Pirouettes: Spinning gracefully on one leg.
